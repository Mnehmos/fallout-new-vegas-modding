# Perk Tier Framework — FNV Perk Overhaul

## Design Philosophy: Build-Defining Moments

> "A great perk doesn't just improve your character — it changes how you play."

FNV should have more moments where taking a perk makes you say *"this is a completely different game now."* The overhaul leans into this hard. We want more S-tier perks, and we want S-tier to mean something.

---

## Revised Tier Definitions

### S Tier — Identity Perks
These perks define *who your character is*. They're the reason you built a certain way. They should be flashy, powerful, and have cascading interactions with other systems.

**S-tier perks should:**
- Have a moment that feels "broken" on first use
- Synergize with at least 2–3 other perks
- Enable a playstyle that literally cannot function at full power without them
- Create memorable moments the player will talk about

**S-tier can be powerful because FNV is already solvable.** The point is to make the *journey* feel like a power fantasy.

### A Tier — Build Anchors
Perks you always take on a qualifying build. Clear payoff, consistent utility. These are the workhorses — they make your S-tier choices even better.

### B Tier — Situational Specialists
Good on the right build. Not every character needs them, but when they fit, they deliver. A full B-tier pick still feels satisfying.

**There is no C tier in the overhaul.** Every perk must reach at least B.

## Tier Goals for Overhaul

| Tier | Vanilla Count | Overhaul Target |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| S    | ~5           | **18–22 perks** |
| A    | ~12          | ~25 perks       |
| B    | ~20          | ~30 perks       |
| C    | ~30          | 0               |
| D/F  | ~20          | 0               |

We're roughly tripling the S-tier count. This is intentional.

---

## What Earns S-Tier in This Overhaul

A perk earns S-tier if it does **at least one** of these:

1. **Multiplier stacking** — Bonus multiplies with other bonuses (crits × sneak attack × perk = massive numbers)
2. **System enabler** — Enables a mechanic that barely functions without it
3. **Reflex-level power** — Creates an instant feedback loop (kill = reward = kill more)
4. **Economy breaker** — Removes a resource constraint that would otherwise cap your power
5. **Threat redefinition** — Changes what threats mean (you don't dodge explosions, you absorb them)

## S-Tier Perk Archetypes

### The Grim Reaper Loop
Perks that create AP/action feedback loops. Kill something → get resources → kill more.
- **Grim Reaper's Sprint** (kill in VATS → full AP)
- **Action Boy/Girl** (more AP = more VATS)
- **Math Wrath** (cheaper VATS = more kills per AP bar)
- **Meltdown** (energy kill → explosion → chain)

### The Crit Stack
Perks whose effects multiply each other.
- **Finesse** (+5% base crit chance)
- **Better Criticals** (+50% crit damage — this MULTIPLIES the already-multiplied crit)
- **Ninja** / **The Professional** (sneak crit multiplier, stacks on top)
- **Sniper** (+25% headshot crits in VATS)

Every one of these is S-tier or near it. Together they're broken. That's the point.

### The Weapon Specialist
Perks that define a weapon-type fantasy.
- **Cowboy** — revolvers, lever-action, knives (the Lone Wanderer)
- **Grunt** — service rifles, SMGs, shotguns (the soldier)
- **Ninja** — all melee/unarmed (the shadow warrior)
- **Commando** / **Gunslinger** — VATS accuracy (the marksman)
- **Meltdown** — energy weapons (the plasma destroyer)
- **Demolition Expert** — explosives (the demolitionist)

Each of these should feel like "I am this archetype now."

### The Tank
Perks that rewrite survivability rules.
- **Nerd Rage!** — glass cannon who unkills themselves under pressure
- **Stonewall** — unmovable, unstoppable in close quarters
- **Toughness** — raw DT, still valuable
- **Adamantium Skeleton** — limb damage becomes irrelevant

### The Ghost
Stealth perks that create an invisible death machine.
- **Ninja** (×25 melee sneak crits — absurd)
- **The Professional** (sneak attack pistol crits)
- **Silent Running** (run silently — the enabler)
- **Ghost** (no movement penalty — tier lift needed)
- **Mister Sandman** (instant kills at night — atmosphere and power)

## Promotion Targets: Perks Being Lifted to S-Tier

These perks were A or B in vanilla and are being **redesigned to S-tier** in the overhaul:

| Perk | Why It Deserves S | Redesign |
|------|------------------|----------|
| **Cowboy** | Should be the defining "gunslinger" perk | Buff to +35% damage; add: drawing a revolver from holster grants 1 free VATS action (the quick draw moment) |
| **Grunt** | Should be the defining "soldier" perk | Buff to +35% damage; add: killing 3 enemies in a row triggers a "suppressing fire" mode — next burst has 0 AP cost |
| **Ninja** | Already S-tier, make it MORE S | Add: successful melee sneak attacks don't break stealth for 3 seconds |
| **The Professional** | Should define the pistolero | Buff to +35% sneak crit; add: pistol/SMG kills in sneak refresh 50% of sneak crit chance instantly |
| **Demolition Expert** | Should define the explosives build | Rank 3 now also: explosions you throw have a 25% chance to trigger a secondary explosion at the impact site |
| **Action Boy/Girl** | Should feel like pure speed | Increase to +35 AP per rank; add: at max rank (70 AP bonus), first VATS use per combat is free |
| **Better Criticals** | Already S, make it legendary | Buff to +75% crit damage; critical kills now leave a brief "crit echo" — next shot within 3 seconds has +20% crit chance |
| **Nerd Rage!** | Should be terrifying to fight | At <20% HP: current effects PLUS you deal +10% damage for every 1% HP below 20% (scales to +200% at 1 HP) |
| **Meltdown** | Should feel apocalyptic | Buff explosion radius by 50%; chained explosions can trigger again off killed-by-explosion targets (true chain reaction) |
| **Slayer** | Should make unarmed feel superhuman | +40% attack speed; add: consecutive hits on same target stack a damage bonus (+5% per hit, up to +50%) |
| **Grim Reaper's Sprint** | Already legendary, stay legendary | No change — the AP loop is already the best feeling in the game |
| **Sniper** | Should define long-range builds | +35% headshot chance; add: headshots that kill instantly cause nearby enemies to make a Morale check (may flee) |
| **Hand Loader** | Economic S-tier | Also add: hand-loaded ammo deals +15% bonus damage on top of normal variant bonuses |
| **Jury Rigging** | Already S-tier economic god | No change needed |
| **Kamikaze** | Needs a redesign to earn S | New: +15 AP, -3 DT. Your VATS actions deal +15% more damage. You go first in any combat (AP regen starts 2s earlier). "Speed is your armor." |
| **Ferocious Loyalty** | Companion builds need an S-tier | Buff: companions gain +50 DT AND +30% damage when you drop below 30% HP; you also regen 2 HP/sec while a companion is fighting nearby |
| **Eye for Eye** | Glass cannon potential | Buff: +15% damage per crippled limb (up from +10%); at 3+ crippled limbs: you ignore all movement penalties and deal +50% damage |

## New S-Tier Candidates (Perks Being Redesigned from D/F)

| Perk | New S-Tier Design |
|------|------------------|
| **Here and Now** | Renamed: **Crucible.** Taking this perk triggers a special challenge encounter immediately — survive it and gain 2 perk points (take 2 more perks right now). Die and lose 1 SPECIAL point permanently. High risk, highest reward. |
| **Terrifying Presence** | Renamed: **The Fear.** In combat, once per fight: if you are at full HP and speak first, ALL enemies make a morale check. Those who fail flee for 30 seconds. Those who don't flee receive a -20 DT debuff for 60 seconds. "Your reputation walks into the room first." |
| **Swift Learner** | Renamed: **Accelerated Growth.** +15% XP per rank AND each rank permanently raises INT by 1. At rank 3: +45% XP, +3 INT. You're not grinding levels — you're *becoming* something. |
| **Explorer** | Renamed: **Pathfinder.** Reveal all locations AND gain "First Blood" — your first attack in any newly discovered location deals 3× damage. Exploration is now aggressive reconnaissance. |

## Evaluation Rubric (Updated)

For each perk, score 1–5:

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Damage Impact | 2× | Direct combat effectiveness |
| Build Synergy | 2× | Multiplies other perk choices |
| Flashiness | 1× | Does it create a memorable moment? |
| Utility Breadth | 1× | Useful in many situations |
| Scaling | 1× | Stays relevant start to end |

**Score → Tier:**
- 32–35: S
- 24–31: A
- 15–23: B
- Below 15: Needs redesign

---

## The Golden Rule

> If a player doesn't feel a perk the moment they take it, it's not working.

Every perk should have an immediate feedback moment. A sound. A visual. A moment where the combat changes. Players should feel the perk, not just read it in their Pip-Boy.
